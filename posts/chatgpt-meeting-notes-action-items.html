ChatGPT Meeting Notes Prompt for Action Items

Updated May 22, 2026 • Meeting workflow • 7 min read

Meeting notes are only valuable if they become decisions and next steps. ChatGPT can turn rough notes or a transcript into a clean follow-up, but the prompt needs to separate real action items from general discussion.

What to prepare

  • Raw notes or transcript, shortened if it is very long.
  • Names of people who attended.
  • Project name and meeting goal.
  • Any deadline rules, such as “anything urgent means this week.”
  • A version with private client, employee, or customer details removed.

The workflow

  1. Paste the notes or transcript. For long transcripts, paste one section at a time and ask ChatGPT to keep a running action-item list.
  2. Ask for decisions, open questions, action items, owners, and deadlines.
  3. Ask ChatGPT to mark unclear owners, vague tasks, and missing deadlines instead of inventing details.
  4. Generate a short follow-up email from the cleaned summary.

Copy this prompt

Turn these meeting notes into a useful work summary. Project: [project name] Meeting goal: [goal] Attendees: [names] Raw notes: [paste notes] Return: 1. Decisions made 2. Action items in a table with task, owner, deadline, and source note 3. Open questions 4. Risks or blockers 5. Items that need confirmation because the notes are unclear 6. A short follow-up email I can send to the group Rules: - Focus only on concrete next steps, not general discussion. - If an owner is unclear, write "Unassigned". - If a deadline is unclear, write "TBD". - Do not invent decisions, promises, or dates. - Keep the follow-up email under 160 words.

Quick quality check

Before sending the result, check whether each action item has a clear verb, owner, deadline, and enough context that someone who missed the meeting would know what to do. If not, ask ChatGPT to rewrite only the weak action items.

Fix a weak output

Review the action items you just created. For each item, check: 1. Is the task specific enough? 2. Is the owner named or clearly marked Unassigned? 3. Is the deadline explicit, inferred from the notes, or marked TBD? 4. Did you separate confirmed decisions from open questions? Rewrite the list and mark anything uncertain as "needs confirmation".

Follow-up email template

After ChatGPT creates the summary, ask it to rewrite the follow-up in a friendly, concise tone. The best email should be short enough that busy people actually read it.

The money value here is not the summary. It is fewer missed tasks, fewer repeated meetings, and faster handoffs.

Best fit

This workflow is useful for project managers, freelancers, agencies, consultants, startup teams, real estate teams, and anyone who ends meetings with scattered notes and no clear owner list.
