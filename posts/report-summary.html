ChatGPT Report Summary Prompt for Decisions and Risks

Updated July 16, 2026 • Office workflow • 9 min read

ChatGPT can shorten a long report, but a useful business summary should do more than reduce word count. It should tell the reader what changed, what matters, what is risky, what decision is needed, and what should happen next.

What to prepare

  • The report, transcript, memo, or rough notes.
  • How you are sharing it: pasted text, PDF upload, transcript, screenshots, tables, or section-by-section chunks.
  • The reader's role: executive, manager, client, team, board, or vendor.
  • The decision or meeting the summary will support.
  • Any known deadlines, constraints, budget limits, or policy rules.
  • Facts that must not be guessed, such as numbers, dates, owners, legal obligations, or financial claims.

Copy this prompt

Turn this report into a decision-ready business brief. Audience: [executive, manager, team, client, board] Purpose of the summary: [what the reader needs to decide, understand, or do] Known context: [project, customer, department, deadline, budget, policy, or meeting context] Source and format: [pasted text, PDF, transcript, screenshots, tables, charts, or section 1 of 3] Facts that must not be guessed: [numbers, owners, dates, obligations, pricing, legal claims, or anything uncertain] Report text or notes: [paste content] Return: 1. Bottom-line summary under 90 words 2. Source check: what content you can read, what you cannot verify, and any tables, charts, captions, or sections that may need manual checking 3. What changed since the last update, if the report says 4. Key findings with the evidence or section they came from 5. Risks, deadlines, obligations, or tradeoffs 6. Decisions needed, with the decision owner if stated 7. Recommended next actions with owner, due date, and confidence level 8. Unsupported claims, missing facts, or places where the report is unclear 9. Questions the reader should ask before acting

Example input

Audience: Operations director Purpose of the summary: Decide whether to approve a customer onboarding delay and what to tell the client. Known context: The project is two weeks from launch. Support training is not finished. The client asked for a status update by Friday. Facts that must not be guessed: Do not invent launch dates, staffing numbers, customer commitments, or legal risk. Report text or notes: [paste the project report or notes]

Expected output

A strong answer should not just say the report is "about onboarding delays." It should name the practical decision, the evidence behind the risk, what is still unknown, who needs to act, and what should not be promised yet.

For sensitive reports, ask ChatGPT to mark uncertainty directly. The summary should separate confirmed facts from assumptions, estimates, and missing information.

If the report is long or hard to read

Do not ask for a final summary until ChatGPT confirms what it can actually read. Long PDFs, copied tables, screenshots, charts, and transcripts can lose structure. If the source is messy, use this check first.

Before summarizing, check whether you can read the source accurately. Source type: [PDF upload, pasted text, transcript, screenshot, table, chart, or section 1 of several] Goal: [what decision or question this summary must support] Content: [paste or upload the content] Return: 1. What parts of the source are readable 2. What parts may be missing, cut off, badly formatted, or unclear 3. Tables, charts, footnotes, captions, or numbers that need manual checking 4. Whether the source should be split into sections before summarizing 5. The exact extra text, page, table, or screenshot you need before writing a final brief Do not write the final summary yet. Do not guess missing numbers, labels, owners, dates, or conclusions.

Repair prompt

Rewrite the summary so it is useful for a busy decision-maker. Fix these problems: - Remove generic restatement of the report - Put the decision or risk in the first paragraph - Separate facts from assumptions - Add source notes for major claims - Mark missing owners, dates, numbers, or evidence - Do not invent deadlines, obligations, or recommendations - End with the next three actions the reader can safely take

Common mistake

Do not accept a summary that only shortens the text. A useful report brief should help someone act without hiding uncertainty, missing evidence, or unclear ownership.
