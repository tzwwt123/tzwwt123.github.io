ChatGPT Prompt for Sales Follow-Up Email After a Proposal

July 19, 2026 • Sales workflow • 6 min read

A proposal follow-up should make the decision easier. This prompt helps ChatGPT summarize the proposal around the buyer's goal, surface the likely objection, and ask for one clear answer without adding pressure.

Copy this prompt

Write a sales follow-up email after sending a proposal. Buyer: [role and company] Proposal sent: [date and what was included] Buyer goal: [what they said they want] Most relevant proposal point: [scope, price, timeline, deliverable, proof, support, or risk] Likely open question or objection: [budget, timing, scope, legal, technical review, internal approval, unclear] Decision I need: [approve, revise scope, involve another person, answer one question, or pause] Facts I can mention: [proposal terms, agreed problem, deadline, proof, or none] Rules: - Do not re-pitch the whole proposal. - Connect the follow-up to the buyer's stated goal. - Do not invent urgency, budget authority, approval status, or savings. - Ask for one clear decision or one small next step. - Keep the email under 130 words. - Include a version that lets the buyer ask for a scope change. Return: 1. Subject line 2. Email body 3. Short version 4. Softer version 5. One-sentence decision ask 6. Risky or unsupported sentence to remove

Example input

Buyer: Founder of a local services company Proposal sent: Website refresh proposal sent Monday with timeline and three milestones Buyer goal: Get more quote requests without hiring another coordinator Most relevant proposal point: Quote-request landing page and follow-up email flow Likely open question or objection: Budget and whether they need all three milestones Decision I need: Ask whether to approve, reduce scope, or schedule a short review Facts I can mention: Proposal includes a smaller phase-one option

What good output should include

  • A short reminder of the proposal's outcome, not all details.
  • A clear decision path: approve, revise, review, or pause.
  • No pressure language.
  • No invented deadline or savings claim.
  • A buyer-friendly way to request a smaller scope.
