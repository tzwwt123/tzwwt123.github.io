ChatGPT Resume Prompt That Does Not Sound Generic

Updated June 7, 2026 • Job search workflow • 8 min read

ChatGPT can help improve a resume, but it should not write one from scratch or turn your experience into vague corporate language. Use it like an editor: show the job description, keep your real details, and ask it to make the most relevant proof easier to see.

What to prepare

  • Your current resume bullets.
  • The job description or the top requirements.
  • Real numbers, tools, results, projects, responsibilities, and context you can defend.
  • Your target level, such as intern, coordinator, associate, manager, or senior.
  • Words, claims, metrics, titles, or tone you do not want ChatGPT to change.

Copy this prompt

Help me tailor my resume to this job without making it sound generic or inflated. Target role: [role] Job description: [paste relevant parts] My current resume bullets: [paste bullets] Real facts I can support: [tools, numbers, results, responsibilities, projects, customers, systems, or examples] Target seniority: [intern, entry-level, coordinator, associate, manager, senior, etc.] Do not change: [metrics, job titles, scope, tools, wording, or facts that must stay accurate] Words or style to avoid: [spearheaded, leveraged, passionate, results-driven, strategic, dynamic, or anything that does not sound like me] Tone: Clear, specific, plain, and human. Do not make me sound more senior than I am. Rules: - Do not invent metrics, tools, promotions, leadership scope, company facts, or outcomes. - Preserve my real numbers and specific achievements unless you explain why a change is needed. - Do not copy the job description word for word. - If a requirement is not supported by my experience, mark it as a gap instead of faking alignment. - Prefer stronger ordering and clearer wording over a full rewrite. Return: 1. The 3 most relevant bullets for this job and why 2. Rewritten bullets under 24 words each 3. Job-description keywords I can honestly include 4. Keywords I should not include because my evidence does not support them 5. Any original detail, metric, or context you preserved 6. Lines that still sound generic, inflated, or AI-written 7. One question I should answer to make the resume stronger

Test input

Target role: Customer success associate Job description: onboard customers, answer product questions, document repeat issues, work with sales and product teams Current resume bullets: - Helped students with account issues at a campus service desk - Made a FAQ for common scheduling questions - Trained new volunteers on the scheduling tool Real facts I can support: trained 12 volunteers, wrote a 2-page FAQ, handled 20 to 30 walk-up questions per shift, used Zendesk Target seniority: entry-level Do not change: 12 volunteers, 20 to 30 questions, Zendesk Words to avoid: spearheaded, strategic, passionate, dynamic

What good output should include

  • Bullets that keep the original facts and make the relevant experience easier to notice.
  • Language that matches the job without copying full phrases from the posting.
  • No fake metrics, inflated leadership claims, or senior-sounding words for junior work.
  • A clear list of unsupported keywords instead of pretending every requirement fits.
  • At least one check for repeated verbs, buzzwords, and generic AI rhythm.

Repair prompt

These resume bullets still sound generic or inflated. Rewrite them again with these rules: - Keep my exact facts, numbers, tools, and seniority level. - Replace broad claims with specific work I actually did. - Remove buzzwords and repeated action verbs. - Do not make the job sound bigger than it was. - Do not copy the job description directly. - After rewriting, list any bullet that still needs a stronger real example.

Common mistake

Do not ask ChatGPT to “make my resume ATS friendly” and accept the whole rewrite. That often creates bland bullets that sound like everyone else. Ask for relevance, gaps, and clearer wording, then keep only changes you can explain in an interview.
