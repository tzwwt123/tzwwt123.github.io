ChatGPT Cover Letter Prompt That Does Not Sound Generic

Updated June 6, 2026 • Job search workflow • 7 min read

ChatGPT can make cover letters less painful, but generic letters are easy to spot. The strongest workflow is to give it the job description, your real experience, and a few specific reasons you want the role.

What to prepare

  • The job description or the top 5 requirements.
  • Two or three real examples from your work, school, volunteer, or project experience.
  • One honest reason this company, team, product, or role interests you.
  • Your resume or a short summary of the experience you can defend in an interview.
  • Words you do not want to use, especially phrases that sound like every other AI cover letter.

Copy this prompt

Draft a cover letter that sounds specific, honest, and not AI-generated. Job title: [title] Company: [company] Job description or top requirements: [paste relevant parts] My relevant evidence: [real examples, projects, results, tools, customers, classes, volunteer work, or responsibilities] Why I want this role: [one real reason, not generic enthusiasm] My normal writing style: [plain, warm, direct, concise, formal, etc.] Phrases to avoid: ["I am excited to apply," "fast-paced environment," "passionate," "uniquely qualified," "dynamic," or any phrase I dislike] Tone: Clear, specific, confident, and natural. Do not sound overly formal, inspirational, salesy, or like LinkedIn. Rules: - Do not invent experience, metrics, company facts, awards, or personal motivation. - Connect each claim to evidence I provided. - If the job description is vague or my evidence is weak, ask for what is missing instead of filling the gap. - Do not repeat my resume line by line. - Keep it short enough for a recruiter to skim. Return: 1. Cover letter under 260 words 2. A shorter email version 3. 3 lines that still sound generic, exaggerated, or AI-written 4. What evidence each paragraph is based on 5. One question I should answer to make the letter more specific

Test input

Job title: Customer success associate Company: Small B2B software company Job description or top requirements: onboarding customers, answering product questions, documenting repeat issues, working with sales and product teams My relevant evidence: helped train 12 new volunteers on a scheduling tool; wrote a simple FAQ that reduced repeat questions; handled frustrated customers at a campus service desk Why I want this role: I like helping people understand tools without making them feel embarrassed My normal writing style: plain and direct Phrases to avoid: passionate, fast-paced, uniquely qualified, thrilled to apply

What good output should include

  • A first sentence that names the role and a real fit, not a generic expression of excitement.
  • One or two concrete examples the applicant can explain later.
  • A clear connection between the job requirements and the applicant's evidence.
  • No inflated claims, fake company knowledge, or resume copy-paste.
  • No empty AI-sounding phrases that could apply to hundreds of applicants.

Repair prompt

This cover letter still sounds generic. Rewrite it with these rules: - Keep only claims supported by my evidence. - Replace broad traits with specific examples. - Remove any sentence that could apply to almost any applicant. - Remove overly polished, inspirational, salesy, or LinkedIn-style language. - Keep my voice plain and natural. - After rewriting, list every sentence that still needs a stronger real detail.

Common mistake

Do not let the letter become a repeat of your resume or a polished paragraph that could fit any job. Ask ChatGPT to connect your real evidence to the employer's needs, then edit the result before sending.
