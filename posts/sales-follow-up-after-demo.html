ChatGPT Prompt for Sales Follow-Up Email After a Demo

July 19, 2026 • Sales workflow • 6 min read

A demo follow-up should prove that you listened. This prompt turns messy demo notes into a short email that repeats the buyer's goal, answers the open concern, and proposes one practical next step.

Copy this prompt

Write a sales follow-up email after a demo. Buyer and company: [role, company type, team size, or use case] Demo context: [what they saw, what they asked about, and who attended] Buyer goal: [what they want to improve or avoid] Questions they asked: [list exact questions] Concerns or objections: [setup, price, security, integrations, team adoption, timing, or unknown] Useful proof or resource: [case study, help article, implementation note, comparison, checklist, or none] Recommended next step: [answer question, send resource, schedule technical review, invite teammate, proposal, or pause] Rules: - Start with the buyer's goal or question from the demo. - Answer the most important open concern. - Do not repeat every feature shown in the demo. - Do not invent numbers, results, testimonials, integrations, or timelines. - If proof is missing, write a lighter email without pretending. - Keep it under 140 words. Return: 1. Subject line 2. Follow-up email 3. Shorter version 4. Follow-up angle supported by the notes 5. Missing fact to confirm before sending 6. Unsupported claim to remove

Example input

Buyer and company: Head of support at a B2B SaaS company Demo context: They saw the help-desk triage workflow and asked about Zendesk setup Buyer goal: Reduce repetitive ticket sorting Questions they asked: Can non-technical admins update categories? Concerns or objections: Setup time and support-team adoption Useful proof or resource: Admin setup checklist, no public case study Recommended next step: Send checklist and ask whether their support ops lead should join a 20-minute setup review

What good output should include

  • The buyer's own demo question.
  • One answer or resource, not a full recap.
  • A next step that fits the buyer's concern.
  • No fake proof or overconfident implementation promise.
  • A note about what should be confirmed.
